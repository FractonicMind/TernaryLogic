# No Spy — No Weapon (NoS-NoW)

> *"A system that cannot be compelled to spy and cannot be weaponized is not a limitation — it is the architecture of trust."*
> — Lev Goukassian

---

## Overview

This folder contains the deep research specifications for the **No Spy, No Weapon (NoS-NoW)** structural mandate of the Ternary Logic (TL) framework.

The NoS-NoW mandate is one of two co-equal, non-bypassable invariants governing all TL-based Global Decision Systems and Operational Systems (OpSy). It establishes categorical structural prohibition on participation in lethal targeting systems, autonomous weapon platforms, and mass civilian surveillance infrastructure — enforced at the architectural layer, not the policy layer.

These prohibitions are not configurable. They cannot be suspended, exempted, or overridden without fundamental system modification.

## Primary Finding

Ternary Logic cannot achieve universal adoption as a global decision-system standard while enforcing both invariants. Three non-negotiable barriers preclude universality:

1. **Jurisdictional incompatibility** — China and Russia require surveillance capabilities Invariant II categorically prohibits (~25–30% of global GDP excluded)
2. **Latency exclusion** — The 150–250 μs cryptographic provenance floor structurally excludes all sub-millisecond deterministic response domains
3. **The Oracle Problem** — Process integrity does not guarantee outcome integrity; external sensor corruption cannot be fully eliminated

The alternative addressable market — institutional finance, CBDC clearing, climate risk, critical infrastructure, strict-liability medical systems — exceeds **$1.2–1.8 trillion**, growing at 15–23% CAGR. Economic viability without prohibited sectors is confirmed.

## Gray Zones Resolved

Seven structural vulnerabilities are identified and reinforced in the primary specification:

1. **Oracle Problem & data provenance spoofing** — Multi-source attestation (N ≥ 3); process integrity ≠ outcome integrity
2. **Statistical inference disguised as deductive engines** — Computational graph type system; probabilistic taint propagation
3. **Dual-use platforms with latent surveillance capability** — Capability-level prohibition; kernel-level query scope limiters
4. **Weapons integration framed as logistics** — Wassenaar Arrangement product-category filtering at data input layer
5. **Defensive cyber operations with offensive potential** — Output classification gates; organizational separation mandate
6. **Epistemic Hold induction attack** — Circuit breaker degradation to Byzantine quorum after threshold *t*
7. **Supply chain compiler compromise** — Reproducible builds; CompCert-style formal compiler verification
