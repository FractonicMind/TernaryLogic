# SECTION 8: HARDWARE ENFORCEMENT

## 8.0 Overview

Every governance framework that relies exclusively on software for enforcement contains a fundamental vulnerability: a sufficiently privileged adversary can modify the software. Root access can patch the operating system. A hypervisor can remap memory. Microcode can be updated. A kernel module can intercept system calls. The institutional weight of a compliance department, the legal deterrence of a regulatory penalty, and the cryptographic strength of a hash chain all reduce to zero if the process generating the hash can be instructed to lie about what it hashed. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

Ternary Logic addresses this vulnerability through hardware enforcement. The constitutional determinations of the Governance Lane are not merely computed. They are physically instantiated in the resistance states of memristive cells, enforced by the handshake protocols of asynchronous ternary logic circuits, and protected by tamper-evident enclosures that detect and respond to physical intrusion. The Epistemic Hold is not a software flag. It is a physical NULL state at half-Vdd in Delay-Insensitive Ternary Logic circuits - a voltage condition that the circuit cannot escape without receiving a verified cryptographic confirm pulse. [[5]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Governance/Governance_Complete.md)

This Section specifies the hardware enforcement architecture of TL in full technical detail. It covers the DITL asynchronous circuit paradigm, the Mandated Ternary memristive switching elements, the Triadic Coprocessor integration requirements, the six mandatory conditions for physical non-bypassability, the fabrication and supply chain risk profile, and the deployment roadmap from software emulation through Architecture B hybrid deployment to Architecture A native ternary commercialization.

The technical standard established in this Section is demanding because the governance claims made by the framework are demanding. A specification that claims physics-level enforcement of constitutional determinations must provide the physics-level technical detail that substantiates that claim. Sections that refer vaguely to "hardware-backed security" without specifying the material composition, circuit topology, and failure modes of the hardware are not technical standards. They are marketing documents. This Section is a technical standard. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

## 8.1 The Enforceability Gap: Why Software Governance Fails

The enforceability gap is the structural chasm between what a governance framework claims to enforce and what it can actually prevent. In software-only governance systems, this gap is unbounded from above: given sufficient privilege and motivation, any software check can be bypassed. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

The attack surface of a software-only governance system includes the operating system kernel, which can be patched to intercept and modify governance API calls; the hypervisor, which can remap the governance module's memory pages to a shadow implementation; the microcode layer of the host CPU, which can alter the behavior of cryptographic instructions; the firmware of peripheral devices with DMA access, which can read and write the governance module's memory directly; the time source, which can be manipulated to produce fraudulent timestamps; and the network stack, which can intercept and modify regulatory reporting transmissions before they leave the institution's infrastructure. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

None of these attack vectors requires physical access to the institution's hardware. Many can be executed remotely by an attacker who has compromised a single privileged credential. In the context of sovereign-grade governance of global economic systems, where the adversary may be a nation-state with signals intelligence capabilities, an insider with system administrator access, or a criminal organization with the resources to purchase zero-day exploits, a governance framework whose enforcement depends entirely on software is not a governance framework. It is a policy statement dressed in technical terminology.

TL's hardware enforcement architecture addresses the enforceability gap by moving the critical governance enforcement points below the software layer - into the domain of physics, where modification requires physical access to fabricated silicon and produces detectable signatures that trigger automatic constitutional safe harbor. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

The residual gap - the set of attacks that cannot be prevented even by hardware enforcement - is defined, bounded, and disclosed. Dopant-level hardware Trojans inserted during fabrication cannot be detected by any post-fabrication verification protocol. Supply chain compromise by a state actor with access to the foundry's mask fabrication process cannot be prevented by the framework specification alone. These residual risks are tracked in the Constitutional Uncertainty Register and managed through multi-vendor diversity, behavioral monitoring, and the institutional governance of the Tri-Cameral model. A specification that claims zero residual risk is not a credible specification. This one does not. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

## 8.2 Delay-Insensitive Ternary Logic: Circuit Architecture

Delay-Insensitive Ternary Logic is the asynchronous hardware substrate that physically enforces the Governance Lane's constitutional determinations. Understanding its architecture requires understanding the distinction between synchronous and asynchronous digital circuits - a distinction that is not merely technical but constitutionally significant. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

### 8.2.1 Synchronous vs. Asynchronous: The Constitutional Significance

In a conventional synchronous digital circuit, a global clock ticks at a fixed frequency. Every gate in the system must settle to a valid output before the clock edge that samples it. All computation is coordinated by this single shared timebase. The clock is not merely a performance parameter. It is a security attack surface. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

An adversary who controls or manipulates the clock can force gates to sample inputs before they have settled, producing metastable outputs that can be steered toward an attacker-preferred state. Glitch attacks - where the power supply is manipulated to cause voltage drops during specific clock cycles - exploit the timing dependency of synchronous logic to skip security checks, flip authentication bits, and corrupt cryptographic state machines. Timing side-channel attacks measure the time required for specific computations to infer secret key material from the statistical correlation between computation time and data value. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

DITL eliminates the global clock entirely. Each gate communicates with its neighbors through local request-acknowledge handshake protocols. A gate asserts its output only when its internal computation is complete. The receiving gate acknowledges only when it has consumed the result and is ready for the next input. No gate ever samples a signal before it has stabilized, because there is no external timing signal to force premature sampling. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md) The circuit's behavior is correct by construction, independent of the propagation delay through any individual gate.

This eliminates the clock-based attack surface entirely. There are no clock edges to exploit for glitch injection. There is no regular timing pattern to measure for side-channel analysis. There is no global synchronization signal that a compromised peripheral can manipulate. The Governance Lane evaluates proposals at the speed of evidentiary verification - as fast as the evidence is complete, and no faster. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

### 8.2.2 The Four-Phase Handshake Protocol

All inter-gate communication in the DITL substrate operates through a four-phase handshake protocol. The protocol governs the transfer of a single data item between a sender gate and a receiver gate. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

**Phase 1 - Data Assertion:** The sender asserts a valid data encoding on its output rails. For 1-of-3 ternary encoding, this means activating exactly one of the three output wires corresponding to the triadic state being transmitted: the Proceed wire, the Refuse wire, or the Epistemic Hold wire. The spacer or NULL state, in which all three wires are inactive, signals that no valid data is present.

**Phase 2 - Acknowledge Assertion:** The receiver detects that all required inputs have transitioned from the NULL spacer state to valid data encodings. When all inputs are valid, the receiver asserts its acknowledge output. This signal propagates back to the sender indicating that the data has been captured.

**Phase 3 - Data Withdrawal:** The sender detects the acknowledge signal and withdraws its data, returning its output rails to the NULL spacer state.

**Phase 4 - Acknowledge Withdrawal:** The receiver detects that the sender has returned to the NULL state and withdraws its own acknowledge signal. Both sender and receiver are now in the neutral state, ready for the next data transfer. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

Only after all four phases complete can a new data transfer begin. This protocol guarantees that no gate ever processes stale data, partial data, or data corrupted by a race condition. In the Governance Lane, this means that a constitutional evaluation cannot proceed on incomplete evidentiary inputs. The handshake forces the lane to wait until all required data has arrived and stabilized before the evaluation logic activates.

### 8.2.3 Encoding Schemes: 1-of-3 Rail Encoding

Two encoding schemes are possible for ternary logic signals: voltage-level encoding and 1-of-3 rail encoding. The specification mandates 1-of-3 rail encoding for all production deployments. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

Voltage-level encoding represents three ternary states as three voltage levels on a single wire: Vdd for Proceed (+1), Vdd/2 for Epistemic Hold (0), and GND for Refuse (-1). This approach minimizes wire count but halves the noise margin between adjacent states relative to binary encoding. The voltage difference between Epistemic Hold (Vdd/2) and Proceed (Vdd) is only half the supply voltage, compared to the full Vdd margin between binary logic levels. A voltage glitch that corrupts the Epistemic Hold state to Proceed - bypassing the constitutional hesitation state - requires only half the energy that would be needed to flip a binary logic level. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

1-of-3 rail encoding uses three wires per ternary trit. Exactly one wire is active for each state: wire 0 active for Refuse (-1), wire 1 active for Proceed (+1), wire 2 active for Epistemic Hold (0). The NULL or spacer state is all-zero: all three wires inactive. This preserves the full noise margin of the process technology for each state transition. A glitch sufficient to corrupt a valid ternary state would need to simultaneously deactivate the correct wire and activate an incorrect wire - a significantly higher energy requirement than simple voltage perturbation. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

Additionally, 1-of-3 encoding makes ternary-to-binary reduction detectable by completion logic. If an adversary attempts to drive a 1-of-3 encoded ternary signal using binary outputs - forcing the Epistemic Hold wire inactive while activating either the Proceed or Refuse wire - the completion detector can distinguish this from a valid ternary transition because the spacer-to-data transition timing differs from the expected 1-of-3 pattern. The completion logic will not assert a done signal for a malformed encoding.

### 8.2.4 Dual-Rail Encoding for Binary-to-Ternary Interface

The Inference Lane operates in conventional binary logic. The Governance Lane operates in 1-of-3 ternary logic. The interface between them requires a protocol translation that does not introduce a security boundary violation. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

Dual-rail encoding provides this interface. Each binary bit in the Decision Proposal is encoded on two wires: one wire asserts when the bit is logic 1, the other wire asserts when the bit is logic 0, and neither wire asserts during the NULL spacer phase. This encoding preserves the four-phase handshake property at the binary-ternary boundary: the ternary Governance Lane can detect the presence of valid binary data by observing that at least one wire of each dual-rail pair has asserted.

The ternary evaluation logic processes the dual-rail encoded inputs and produces 1-of-3 ternary outputs. The output travels through the Governance Lane's ternary evaluation pipeline, eventually reaching the Gateway. At the Gateway, the 1-of-3 ternary output drives the Mandated Ternary memristive cells. The dual-rail encoding preserves the physical separation between the binary Inference Lane and the ternary Governance Lane at the electron level: neither lane can force the other lane's logic states without producing encoding violations that are detected by the handshake completion logic. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

### 8.2.5 Muller C-Elements and Pipeline Stalls

The Muller C-element is the fundamental building block of DITL pipeline stall logic. It is a hysteresis-state threshold gate whose output remains stable until all its inputs have reached a common logic level. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

In a two-input C-element: the output asserts to logic 1 when both inputs are logic 1. The output asserts to logic 0 when both inputs are logic 0. If the inputs disagree - one at logic 1 and the other at logic 0 - the output remains in its previous state, whatever that was. This hysteresis property creates a pipeline stall: a pipeline stage protected by a C-element cannot advance until all upstream stages have completed and all required inputs have settled to valid levels.

In the DITL Governance Lane, C-elements create the physical implementation of the Epistemic Hold stall. When the Governance Lane has issued Epistemic Hold on a proposal, the C-elements in the pipeline stage guarding the Gateway acknowledge path remain in their current state. The acknowledge signal does not propagate forward. Without the acknowledge signal, the upstream evaluation pipeline cannot advance. Without the upstream pipeline advancing, the Permission Token cannot be generated. Without the Permission Token, the Gateway cannot open. The constitutional hesitation is instantiated in the hysteretic memory of individual circuit elements. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

Bypassing a C-element stall requires physical-layer intervention: injecting the missing acknowledge signal electrically, physically rerouting the signal path on the substrate, or voltage glitching to force the C-element past its hysteresis threshold. The faulTPM class of attacks demonstrates that this is possible with equipment costing as little as fifty dollars for simple CMOS circuits. The specification mitigates this through the active tamper detection infrastructure described in Section 8.5, which detects the electrical signatures of glitch injection and triggers immediate key zeroization and safe harbor transition. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

The C-element stall mechanism is fundamentally different from and stronger than the non-maskable interrupt mechanisms of conventional processor security. The x86 NMI is maskable by setting bit 7 of I/O port 0x70. ARM FIQ is maskable via the F bit in CPSR. ARM's own documentation acknowledges that non-maskable is a misnomer, since NMIs can be masked. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md) Asynchronous handshake stalls operate on a different principle entirely. Without the acknowledge transition, the pipeline physically cannot advance. There is no register to flip, no clock edge to exploit, no port to write. The stall is the circuit's natural resting state when inputs are incomplete.

### 8.2.6 Completion Detection

Completion detection is the mechanism that signals when an entire arithmetic or logic operation - potentially spanning dozens of pipeline stages - has finished. In DITL, a completion detector monitors the output rails of a computational block and asserts a done signal only when all outputs have settled to valid, non-NULL encodings. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

For the Governance Lane, completion detection corresponds to the moment when all evidentiary predicates have been evaluated, all chamber signatures have been verified, and the triadic state determination has stabilized throughout the pipeline. The Gateway does not attempt to open until the completion detector asserts its done signal. The completion detector is the hardware realization of NL=NA: the log must be complete, the token must be valid, and the evaluation pipeline must have fully settled before execution is physically enabled.

The completion detector's done signal is the input to the MT gate's confirm pulse generation logic. Without a valid done signal from the completion detector, the confirm pulse cannot be generated. Without the confirm pulse, the MT gate cannot transition from the intermediate resistance Epistemic Hold state to the low resistance Proceed state. The physical chain from evidentiary completeness to execution authorization is unbroken and hardware-enforced at every link. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

### 8.2.7 Inline vs. Sidecar Placement

The placement of the DITL Governance Lane relative to the host CPU's instruction execution path is constitutionally critical. Two topologies are possible: inline placement and sidecar placement. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

In **inline placement**, the DITL coprocessor sits directly in the critical execution path between instruction decode and instruction commit. The host CPU physically cannot fetch, decode, or commit an execution instruction without passing through the DITL governance bus. If the DITL coprocessor issues Epistemic Hold, the handshake stall propagates directly into the CPU's pipeline, preventing instruction commit until the stall is resolved. This is the only topology that achieves physical non-bypassability.

In **sidecar placement**, the DITL coprocessor sits parallel to the CPU's execution path and communicates via an interrupt or exception mechanism. The CPU continues to execute instructions speculatively while waiting for the DITL result. If the DITL coprocessor issues Epistemic Hold after the CPU has speculatively executed past the governance check, the speculative execution must be rolled back. But speculative execution windows can be enormous: TPM ECDSA signature operations take approximately two hundred milliseconds. If DITL validation latency were comparable, the speculative execution window would encompass tens of millions of CPU cycles. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

More critically, sidecar placement is vulnerable to Spectre-class speculative execution attacks. If DITL validation occurs after the host CPU's instruction execution, the CPU can and will speculatively execute past the DITL validation check before the result arrives. Speculative side effects - cache line residency, branch predictor state, TLB state - can persist even after a speculative rollback, leaking information about the speculative execution to an attacker who can observe these microarchitectural side effects. Only inline placement prevents this class of attack, because the CPU physically cannot speculate past a gate that has not yet asserted its acknowledge signal. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

The specification mandates inline placement for all production deployments of the Triadic Coprocessor. DITL validation latency in inline mode is expected to be in the nanosecond to low-microsecond range for Fast Lane operations, based on the expected pipeline depth and the memristive cell's switching speed. The exact latency is unquantifiable without a fabricated chip - this remains an entry in the Constitutional Uncertainty Register - but DITL's handshake-based protocol operates at the circuit's natural settling speed, which is fundamentally faster than the interrupt-based communication of sidecar placement. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

## 8.3 Mandated Ternary Memristive Cells

The Mandated Ternary memristive cell is the physical switching element that converts constitutional determinations into electrical reality. It is not a software abstraction layered over conventional binary transistors. It is a three-state resistive memory device whose stable resistance regimes correspond exactly to the triadic states of Ternary Logic. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

### 8.3.1 Material Physics and Resistance States

The MT cell operates on the principle of electrically controlled resistance switching in a thin-film metal-oxide stack. The current baseline specification references the TaOx bilayer memristive device architecture, which has demonstrated reliable multi-state switching suitable for ternary logic implementation. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

The device maintains three stable resistance regimes:

**High Resistance State (HRS):** Corresponds to Refuse (-1). Resistance in the range of 1 MOhm to 10 MOhm. The conductive filament in the oxide layer is fully ruptured. No current flows to the execution circuit. The gateway is blocked.

**Intermediate Resistance State (IRS):** Corresponds to Epistemic Hold (0). Resistance in the range of 10 kOhm to 100 kOhm - the half-Vdd state referenced throughout this specification. The conductive filament is partially formed. Insufficient current flows to activate the execution circuit but the device is not in the fully ruptured HRS configuration. This is the metastable deliberation state from which the cell can transition to either Proceed or Refuse upon receiving the appropriate cryptographic confirm pulse.

**Low Resistance State (LRS):** Corresponds to Proceed (+1). Resistance below 1 kOhm. The conductive filament is fully formed. Sufficient current flows to activate the execution circuit. The gateway opens. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

The exact material composition of the MT cell - the specific metal-oxide stack, filament formation mechanism, and electrode materials - remains unspecified at this specification version and is tracked as an immediate-resolution entry in the Constitutional Uncertainty Register. The specification mandates that the chosen material must demonstrate stable intermediate resistance over a minimum of one billion switching cycles, retention of resistance state over a minimum of twenty years at 85 degrees Celsius as validated by Arrhenius acceleration testing, and resistance to voltage glitch-induced state corruption consistent with the noise margin requirements of 1-of-3 rail encoding. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

### 8.3.2 Hysteresis and Non-Volatility

Two physical properties of the MT cell are constitutionally significant: hysteresis and non-volatility.

**Hysteresis** means that the resistance state of the cell depends on the history of current that has passed through it. Transitioning from the Intermediate Resistance State to the Low Resistance State requires a specific programming current that exceeds the threshold for filament completion. A voltage perturbation below this threshold does not budge the cell. A glitch that might corrupt a binary flip-flop - which has no memory of past state and can be flipped by any event that pushes the node voltage past the switching threshold - merely dissipates against the MT cell's hysteretic barrier. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

**Non-volatility** means that the resistance state persists without power. When the facility loses electrical power during a sovereign debt crisis, when a cyberattack induces a system reboot, or when a maintenance cycle requires shutdown, the MT cells retain their states. When power returns, the Gateway resumes from exactly the state it was in before the interruption. A proposal that was in Epistemic Hold at the moment of power loss remains in Epistemic Hold. A proposal that had received Proceed and was awaiting execution timing remains authorized. A proposal that had been Refused remains blocked. The institution cannot claim that a power outage justified bypassing governance because the governance state is physically stored in the non-volatile memristive substrate. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

### 8.3.3 The Cryptographic Confirm Pulse

The state transition of the MT cell from the Intermediate Resistance State to the Low Resistance State is not driven by a simple voltage pulse. It is driven by a patterned voltage sequence - the cryptographic confirm pulse - that encodes the Permission Token's evidentiary payload in a form that the MT gate's analog decoder circuit can verify. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

The confirm pulse is generated by the Permission Token serialization logic downstream of the Governance Lane's five-layer evaluation. The pulse encodes the token header hash and the Tri-Cameral signature aggregate as a patterned voltage sequence whose amplitude, duration, and phase modulation are determined by the cryptographic output of the evaluation. The MT gate's decoder circuit compares the received pulse pattern against the constitutional schema fingerprint stored in a write-once register programmed during post-fabrication enrollment. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

If the pulse pattern matches, the decoder drives a programming current through the MT cell sufficient to form the conductive filament and transition to the Low Resistance Proceed state. If the pulse pattern does not match - because the Permission Token is invalid, the Tri-Cameral signatures are incomplete, or the schema fingerprint has been tampered with - the decoder does not drive the programming current. The cell remains in the Intermediate Resistance Epistemic Hold state.

Even a compromised process with root access to the Gateway server cannot force the MT cell to transition to Proceed because the cell does not respond to software commands. It responds only to the hardware-decoded cryptographic pulse. The software cannot generate a valid pulse because the pulse pattern is derived from cryptographic material that is architecturally inaccessible to general-purpose software execution environments. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

### 8.3.4 The 1T1R Cell Architecture

The baseline MT cell implementation uses a one-transistor-one-resistor (1T1R) architecture. The transistor serves as the access device that controls the programming current through the memristive element. The transistor's gate voltage determines whether programming current is permitted to flow. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

The 1T1R architecture provides several constitutional benefits. The access transistor prevents sneak path currents that would corrupt neighboring cells in a crossbar array. It allows precise control of the programming current magnitude, which is critical for reliable intermediate resistance state targeting. It provides a natural isolation mechanism between the confirm pulse decoder circuit and the memristive element: the decoder must assert a valid gate voltage on the access transistor before any programming current can flow, adding an additional validation layer between cryptographic verification and physical state transition. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

The named fabrication baseline for Architecture B deployment is the TSMC N2 CoWoS ReRAM 1T1R 2025 process design kit. This baseline provides the material specification, design rules, and process parameters against which the MT cell's Arrhenius retention testing, cycling endurance testing, and resistance distribution verification are conducted. Deviation from this baseline requires Technical Council re-certification of all characterization data. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

## 8.4 The Triadic Coprocessor: Six Mandatory Conditions

The Triadic Coprocessor is the physical instantiation of the Governance Lane. It is not a software module running on a general-purpose CPU. It is not a firmware extension of the host processor. It is a dedicated asynchronous ternary logic ASIC that operates as a physically independent coprocessor with its own power domain, clockless handshake protocol, and tamper-resistant enclosure. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

Six mandatory conditions define the minimum specification for any production Triadic Coprocessor. A deployment that does not satisfy all six conditions does not achieve physical non-bypassability and must be classified as operating in Transitional Emulation Mode with the degraded survivability ratings specified in Section 10. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

**Condition 1: Physically Independent ASIC.** The coprocessor must be implemented as a separate ASIC on a separate die from the host CPU. Integration into the host CPU die is prohibited because cross-die fault injection, microcode manipulation, and hypervisor escape become viable attack vectors that undermine the governance architecture. The coprocessor communicates with the host through a dedicated, bus-isolated channel that does not traverse the host CPU's memory management unit, cache hierarchy, or interrupt controller. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

## 8.5 Physical Security and Tamper Detection

The Triadic Coprocessor's physical security infrastructure operates at four distinct detection layers, each targeting a different class of physical attack. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

**Active Tamper Detection Mesh.** A conductive mesh of fine wires is embedded in the packaging layer directly above the active silicon. The mesh forms a continuous electrical circuit that is continuously monitored for resistance changes. Any attempt to open the enclosure, drill through the packaging, desolder the coprocessor from its substrate, or insert probing equipment between the package and the board produces a resistance change that triggers immediate key zeroization and safe harbor transition. The mesh is powered by an independent on-die power supply with a dedicated battery backup, ensuring that the tamper detection remains active even when the main system power is removed.

**On-Die Voltage Monitors.** Dedicated analog voltage monitoring circuits continuously measure the supply voltage against reference thresholds calibrated to the process technology's operational specifications. Voltage glitching equipment - commercially available for as little as fifty dollars - attempts to cause setup and hold time violations in CMOS logic by dropping the supply voltage during specific computational operations. The on-die monitors detect voltage deviations exceeding a defined tolerance band, typically plus or minus five percent of nominal Vdd, and trigger a non-maskable safe harbor interrupt. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md) These monitors are implemented as dedicated analog circuits with independent power supplies that cannot be disabled by software, firmware, or hypervisor commands.

**Temperature Sensors.** On-die temperature sensors detect both extreme cold attacks - where liquid nitrogen or compressed air is used to slow ionic diffusion and preserve DRAM contents for cold boot key extraction - and extreme heat attacks, where elevated temperature is used to accelerate electromigration and destabilize circuit operation. The sensors trigger safe harbor transition when temperature exceeds the operational range of negative forty to positive eighty-five degrees Celsius or drops below the minus forty degree cold attack threshold. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

**Electromagnetic Anomaly Detectors.** On-die EM field sensors detect electromagnetic fault injection - a contactless attack technique that uses focused electromagnetic pulses to induce fault currents in specific circuit regions without requiring physical contact with the device. EMFI equipment costing as little as one hundred dollars has achieved success rates of twenty-two percent against AMD Secure Processor boot sequences in published research. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md) The EM anomaly detectors monitor for field strength anomalies above a threshold calibrated to distinguish operational electromagnetic interference from targeted fault injection pulses.

When any sensor triggers, the response is immediate and cascaded:

1. All pending execution authorizations are cancelled
2. All ephemeral cryptographic key material in SRAM buffers is overwritten with random data
3. The MT cells are driven to the High Resistance Refuse state
4. The Gateway is locked
5. A tamper event record is generated and queued for Immutable Ledger anchoring as soon as the safe harbor state is established
6. The Stewardship Custodians are notified through the authenticated out-of-band monitoring channel [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

The coprocessor cannot be restored to operational status after a tamper event without physical replacement and re-enrollment through the Tri-Cameral post-fabrication attestation protocol. This ensures that a tamper attempt produces a permanent, detectable, and irreversible consequence - not merely a momentary interruption that the adversary can survive by timing their attack around the detection window.

**JTAG and Debug Port Fusing.** JTAG provides direct read and write access to chip internals via the Test Access Port. Most modern chips support JTAG lock bits, but voltage glitching can downgrade protection levels: the faulTPM attack demonstrated that TPM security can be compromised with fifty-dollar equipment and moderate expertise. The Triadic Coprocessor must fuse its JTAG port at the mask level during production, rendering it permanently inaccessible without focused ion beam milling at a cost exceeding one hundred thousand dollars per chip. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md) Any future debugging must occur through a dedicated secure test interface that requires simultaneous physical presence of multiple Stewardship Custodian hardware tokens.

## 8.6 Architecture A and Architecture B: Deployment Roadmap

The TL hardware deployment roadmap defines two distinct architecture generations with different technology readiness levels, deployment timelines, and constitutional capability profiles. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

### 8.6.1 Architecture B: Hybrid Memristive-CMOS (2026-2027 Commercialization Target)

Architecture B is the recommended deployment architecture for the 2026 to 2027 commercialization window. It is a hybrid design that combines conventional CMOS digital logic for the DITL handshake protocol and evaluation logic with memristive MT cells for the execution gateway. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

In Architecture B, the DITL handshake and evaluation logic are implemented using conventional CMOS digital cells from the TSMC N2 process design kit. The C-elements, completion detectors, and four-phase handshake controllers use standard CMOS gate implementations. The MT cells are integrated as a dedicated memristive layer in the back-end-of-line stack, connected to the CMOS logic through dedicated programming and sensing circuits. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

This hybrid approach is recommended because it allows the use of mature, well-characterized CMOS fabrication processes for the logic circuits - reducing fabrication risk, improving yield, and enabling access to the extensive CMOS cell library ecosystem - while reserving the novel memristive technology for the specific function where it is constitutionally essential: the physical instantiation of the ternary execution gate.

Architecture B targets the IEC 61508 SIL 3 certification path with a target completion date of Q4 2027. SIL 3 requires a probability of dangerous failure per hour of between ten to the negative seventh and ten to the negative eighth, a hardware fault tolerance of one (meaning the system can tolerate a single hardware failure without dangerous behavior), and a safe failure fraction exceeding ninety percent. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

The kill criteria for Architecture B development - the conditions under which the development program must be halted and the architecture re-evaluated - are:

- MT cell intermediate resistance state variance exceeding three sigma across a wafer lot, indicating insufficient process control for reliable ternary state targeting
- Arrhenius retention testing predicting less than twenty-year state retention at eighty-five degrees Celsius
- Cycling endurance below one billion write cycles at the target programming current
- Confirm pulse decoder circuit failing to reject invalid pulse patterns with a false acceptance rate exceeding ten to the negative ninth per operation
- PUF bit error rate after error correction exceeding ten to the negative sixth, indicating insufficient process variation entropy for reliable attestation [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

A single permitted recursion is allowed in the Architecture B development program: if any single kill criterion is triggered, one redesign iteration addressing that specific criterion may proceed before escalating to the Technical Council for full architecture re-evaluation. A second trigger of any kill criterion, or a simultaneous trigger of two or more criteria, mandates immediate Technical Council review and Stewardship Custodian notification.

### 8.6.2 Architecture A: Native Ternary Crossbar (2029-2030 Standardization Horizon)

Architecture A is the target long-term architecture. It is a native ternary crossbar compute-in-memory design in which the MT cell array serves not only as the execution gateway but also as the primary computational substrate for the DITL evaluation logic itself. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

In Architecture A, the crossbar array performs ternary matrix-vector multiplication operations directly in the resistance domain, without converting data to digital signals for processing in conventional CMOS logic. Ternary inputs drive row word lines; resistance states of MT cells at each crossbar intersection perform the ternary multiply operation; column sense amplifiers read out the accumulated ternary result. This compute-in-memory paradigm eliminates the data movement bottleneck between logic and memory that limits the performance of conventional digital architectures.

For TL governance applications, Architecture A's constitutional significance is the elimination of the CMOS-to-memristive interface that Architecture B requires. In Architecture B, there is a design boundary between the CMOS logic and the MT cells across which conventional electronic signals must be converted to memristive programming pulses and back. This interface is a potential attack surface: an adversary who can manipulate signals at this boundary can potentially substitute fabricated programming signals for legitimate confirm pulses. Architecture A eliminates this boundary by performing the constitutional evaluation and the physical state transition in the same substrate. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md)

Architecture A is designated FUTURE. Its 2029 to 2030 standardization horizon is contingent on the maturation of large-wafer ternary semiconductor manufacturing. Samsung and UNIST demonstrated the first large-wafer ternary semiconductor in 2019. Carbon nanotube ternary logic reached peer-reviewed publication in January 2025. No commercial-scale ternary manufacturing exists as of the date of this specification. The progression from laboratory demonstration to commercial production requires process yield improvement, design rule development, EDA tool support, and qualification against relevant reliability standards - a process that historically requires five to seven years for novel semiconductor technologies. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

### 8.6.3 FPGA Prohibition

FPGA implementation is categorically prohibited for production deployment of the Triadic Coprocessor. FPGA may be used for development, testing, and simulation only, under conditions explicitly disclosed as Transitional Emulation Mode with full acknowledgment of the associated survivability degradation. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

The prohibition is grounded in demonstrated security failures. Ender, Moradi, and Paar achieved a complete break of Xilinx 7-Series bitstream encryption. Bitstream extraction via thermal laser stimulation takes under seven hours. Four demonstrated case studies on OpenTitan FPGA implementation achieved signal trace extraction from AES cores, secret key extraction via UART, and secret key replacement. FPGA reverse-engineering costs one thousand to ten thousand dollars compared to ASIC reverse-engineering at fifty thousand to two hundred fifty thousand dollars. ## 8.7 Supply Chain Risk and Fabrication Integrity

The Triadic Coprocessor is only as trustworthy as the supply chain that produces it. Constitutional architecture that is defeated by fabrication compromise is not a constitutional architecture. It is a blueprint. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

### 8.7.1 Dopant-Level Hardware Trojans

Dopant-level hardware Trojans represent the most insidious fabrication threat and the irreducible risk that no specification can eliminate. These modifications require zero additional gates, zero additional wires, and zero layout overhead. They alter the dopant polarity in existing transistors to weaken cryptographic implementations, introduce timing faults, or create covert channels - all without changing any visible chip geometry. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

Standard optical reverse engineering cannot detect these modifications because the transistor dimensions, metal layers, and overall layout remain identical to the original design. Destructive reverse engineering at fifty thousand to five hundred thousand dollars per chip is infeasible for multi-billion-transistor designs. For a design with over ten billion transistors, a single-gate insertion represents less than 0.000001 percent area change, below the practical detection threshold of automated GDSII comparison tools. The GDSII file is the complete chip blueprint delivered from the design house to the foundry. The most vulnerable point in the fabrication pipeline is after GDSII delivery but before mask fabrication, when foundry engineers have full access to the design for optical proximity correction. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

### 8.7.2 Geopolitical Concentration Risk

TSMC produces approximately ninety-two percent of the world's most advanced semiconductors. This single-firm concentration creates a critical vulnerability under any scenario where Taiwan's geopolitical status disrupts fabrication capacity or corporate independence. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

The CHIPS Act is accelerating domestic US fabrication capacity: TSMC Arizona Fab 3 targeting 2nm production arrives in 2029, and Intel 18A is ramping with yields of fifty-five to sixty-five percent, below the seventy to eighty percent threshold for commercial profitability. Meaningful US-based 2nm-class capacity arrives in the 2027 to 2029 window. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

The Foreign Direct Product Rule extends US jurisdiction to virtually all advanced semiconductor designs because all major EDA tools - Cadence, Synopsys, Mentor Graphics - are US-origin. A custom asynchronous ternary logic coprocessor designed anywhere in the world using these tools is potentially subject to US export control. A deployment entity that is placed on the Entity List cannot obtain the DITL chip regardless of where the chip is manufactured. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

### 8.7.3 Mitigation Strategy

Four mitigations reduce but do not eliminate supply chain risk.

**Multi-vendor production** reduces the probability of correlated compromise: if two vendors use different process design kits, different EDA tool versions, and different third-party IP blocks, a Trojan inserted at one vendor affects only chips from that vendor. However, if both vendors use the same EDA tools, the same foundry's base IP, or the same process, the correlated compromise risk returns.

**Behavioral monitoring in deployment** detects anomalous chip behavior that may indicate Trojan activation: unexpected timing variations, unauthorized state transitions, or anomalous power consumption patterns. The Technical Council maintains baseline behavioral profiles for each enrolled coprocessor instance and monitors for deviations. However, behavioral monitoring cannot detect dormant Trojans that activate only under specific trigger conditions not encountered during the monitoring period. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

**Post-fabrication quarantine** requires that newly fabricated DITL coprocessors undergo a minimum thirty-day behavioral monitoring period before they are authorized to issue Permission Tokens. During quarantine, the chip operates in audit-only mode: evaluating proposals and generating Decision Logs but withholding Permission Tokens. The Technical Council monitors for anomalies against the expected baseline.

**Foundry diversity and civilian end-use certification** requires the Stewardship Custodians to maintain a vendor exclusion list prohibiting fabrication by entities with defense revenue above a defined threshold. The exact threshold remains an open parameter in the Constitutional Uncertainty Register pending Technical Council determination.

The probability of reliable DITL chip production at scale without detectable compromise is estimated at fifteen to twenty-five percent within a five-year horizon and forty to fifty-five percent within a ten-year horizon, conditioned on successful tape-out at a trusted foundry, multi-vendor production, and domestic fabrication capacity at advanced nodes. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md) These are conditional projections, not empirical measurements. They represent the framework's honest assessment of the hardware production challenge, not its dismissal of the challenge.
