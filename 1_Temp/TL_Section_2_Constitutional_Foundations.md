# SECTION 2: CONSTITUTIONAL FOUNDATIONS

## 2.1 The Goukassian Vow as Foundational Architecture

The Goukassian Vow is the constitutional bedrock of Ternary Logic. Its text is immutable:

> *"Pause when truth is uncertain. Refuse when harm is clear. Proceed where truth is."* [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

These three mandates do not function as optional ethical guidance, internal policy, or configurable parameters. They operate as binding architectural law. Every component of the TL framework - from memristive hardware gates to governance councils - derives its authority, structure, and operational boundaries from this tripartite covenant. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

The Vow establishes a foundational inversion of conventional automated governance. In binary systems, governance is applied after decisions execute: logs are reviewed, anomalies are flagged, and regulators reconstruct intent from fragmented, often self-reported records. The Vow rejects this architecture entirely. Governance under TL is a pre-execution veto mechanism. Binary inference engines propose actions based on pattern recognition, statistical optimization, and historical data. The Governance Lane evaluates whether those proposals satisfy the Vow before any execution occurs. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md) Binary proposes. TL dictates.

Each mandate of the Vow maps directly and exclusively to one of three constitutional decision states:

- "Proceed where truth is" maps to **Proceed (+1)**
- "Refuse when harm is clear" maps to **Refuse (-1)**
- "Pause when truth is uncertain" maps to **Epistemic Hold (0)** [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

This mapping is not metaphorical. It is operational. The triadic states are the computational realization of the Vow, and every mechanism in the TL architecture exists to ensure that the mapping holds under all conditions - including adversarial pressure, institutional panic, and sovereign coercion. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

The Vow also establishes the moral and logical foundation for the Tri-Cameral Governance Model. The Technical Council, Stewardship Custodians, and Smart Contract Treasury each derive their authority from a specific mandate of the Vow. [[3]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Governance/Ternary_Logic_as_Constitutional_Triadic_Governance_Architecture_for_High-Stakes_Automated_Decision-Making.md) The Technical Council ensures that truth is established before Proceed is issued. The Stewardship Custodians evaluate whether harm is clear and execute Refuse when necessary. The Smart Contract Treasury manages the resources and incentives that keep the system operational during Epistemic Hold. No single chamber can override the Vow. All three must operate within its boundaries for any state transition to be constitutionally valid. [[3]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Governance/Ternary_Logic_as_Constitutional_Triadic_Governance_Architecture_for_High-Stakes_Automated_Decision-Making.md)

Because the Vow is architectural law, it is not subject to configuration, parameter tuning, or institutional override. A central bank cannot tune the Vow to approve emergency liquidity injections without evidentiary validation. A trading firm cannot configure the Vow to ignore Epistemic Hold during volatile market conditions. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md) The Vow is the fixed point around which all economic governance rotates. It is the axiom that makes TL a constitutional framework rather than a compliance tool.

## 2.2 The Three Economic Decision States

Ternary Logic operates with a complete and closed decision alphabet of three states: **Proceed (+1)**, **Refuse (-1)**, and **Epistemic Hold (0)**. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md) No economic action within the TL framework may resolve to any state outside this triad. The system does not output probabilistic confidence scores, risk-adjusted recommendations, or graded advisories. It issues constitutional determinations. Every proposal that enters the Governance Lane must exit as one of these three states, and only one. The Gateway recognizes no intermediate or ambiguous signals. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

### 2.2.1 Proceed (+1)

Proceed is the authorization state. It signifies that the Governance Lane has verified sufficient evidentiary certainty, that no Refuse condition has been triggered, and that the proposal satisfies the Goukassian Vow in full. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

A Proceed determination is not a prediction of success. It is a certification that the decision to act is constitutionally defensible. The Permission Token issued alongside Proceed contains the complete Decision Log, the evidentiary hash chain, the schema validation checksum, and the timestamped cryptographic signatures from the relevant governance chambers. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md) Without this token, Proceed is not actionable. The Gateway will not open on a Proceed determination that lacks a valid, fully-signed Permission Token. The state and the token are inseparable.

### 2.2.2 Refuse (-1)

Refuse is the prohibition state. It signifies that the proposal has violated a constitutional boundary, triggered a harm condition under the Goukassian Vow, or failed a mandatory evidentiary test. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

Refuse is absolute. It is not a suggestion to reconsider, a lowered priority flag, or a conditional veto. It is a binding constitutional block. When the Governance Lane issues Refuse, the Gateway enters a locked state. The proposal is permanently barred from execution unless an entirely new evidentiary submission is processed through the full Governance Lane from the beginning. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

The Decision Log for a Refuse determination must document the specific constitutional gate violated, the evidentiary basis for that violation, and the chamber signatures that validated the determination. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md) This documentation serves two purposes: regulatory defensibility, so that institutions can demonstrate to supervisors that they detected and blocked harmful actions; and anti-drift enforcement, so that boundary definitions cannot be gradually weakened through precedent erosion over time.

### 2.2.3 Epistemic Hold (0)

Epistemic Hold is the hesitation state. It is the most consequential and most commonly misunderstood of the three determinations.

Epistemic Hold is **never** an error, null return, timeout, retry condition, or system malfunction. It is a first-class constitutional determination that truth has not yet been established. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md) When the Governance Lane issues Epistemic Hold, it is rendering a constitutional verdict: the available evidence is insufficient to support Proceed, and no harm condition has been detected to trigger Refuse. The proposal exists in a state of deliberate, documented, constitutionally defensible suspension.

This distinction is not merely semantic. It has direct legal consequences. Under the Goukassian Vow, an institution that issues Epistemic Hold has demonstrated that it exercised due care by pausing rather than acting recklessly under conditions of uncertainty. The Epistemic Hold record in the Decision Log is evidence of fiduciary prudence, not evidence of system failure. It transforms what would otherwise appear as inaction into documented constitutional diligence. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

The physical realization of Epistemic Hold in the hardware substrate is equally precise. In Delay-Insensitive Ternary Logic circuits, the NULL or Spacer state is represented by both dual-rail wires held at logic low, denoted as {0,0}. This is the electrical condition that corresponds to the mandatory hesitation state. No valid token propagates past a stage in the NULL state. No execution signal reaches the actuator. [[5]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Governance/Governance_Complete.md) A sufficiently capable adversary cannot argue with a threshold gate. This is what separates TL from every other governance framework: ethics enforced by physics, not policy. The full hardware architecture is specified in Section 8.

The triadic architecture prevents the binary fallacy that every proposal must either succeed or fail immediately. In high-stakes economic governance, the correct response to genuine uncertainty is constitutionally mandated suspension, not probabilistic execution. A trading algorithm that cannot verify the authenticity of a market-moving sovereign announcement should not execute on probability. It should enter Epistemic Hold until the Sacred Pause resolves the evidentiary gap. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md) These suspensions are not system failures. They are constitutional successes.

## 2.3 Epistemic Hold and Sacred Pause: State Distinguished from Workflow

Epistemic Hold and Sacred Pause are not synonyms. They are architecturally distinct concepts that operate in close coordination. Epistemic Hold is a state. Sacred Pause is a workflow. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md) Conflating them produces implementation errors that compromise the entire evidentiary framework.

A state is a static, immutable constitutional determination recorded in the Decision Log at the moment of issuance. A workflow is a dynamic sequence of actions designed to resolve the uncertainty that triggered the state. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md) Epistemic Hold answers the question "What is the constitutional determination?" Sacred Pause answers the question "What happens operationally while that determination holds?"

The Sacred Pause workflow contains several mandatory stages. First, the Governance Lane broadcasts the Epistemic Hold determination to all relevant governance chambers. The Technical Council receives notification that a proposal has entered evidentiary suspension. The Stewardship Custodians are alerted that a constitutional gate has not been satisfied. The Smart Contract Treasury allocates resources to sustain the extended evaluation period. [[3]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Governance/Ternary_Logic_as_Constitutional_Triadic_Governance_Architecture_for_High-Stakes_Automated_Decision-Making.md)

Second, the system queries supplementary data sources. If the uncertainty stems from a missing data element, the workflow requests it through a cryptographically signed API call to the relevant authority. If the uncertainty stems from an anomalous transaction pattern, the workflow invokes the correspondent verification network. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

Third, the Sacred Pause escalates to human oversight if the uncertainty persists beyond configurable constitutional thresholds. These thresholds are not performance timeouts. They are governance parameters set by the Tri-Cameral model and anchored in the constitutional schema. [[3]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Governance/Ternary_Logic_as_Constitutional_Triadic_Governance_Architecture_for_High-Stakes_Automated_Decision-Making.md) A threshold might specify that any Epistemic Hold exceeding four hours on a central bank liquidity proposal must trigger mandatory review by the Stewardship Custodians. Another might specify that any Epistemic Hold exceeding one business day on a high-value cross-border settlement must trigger a full Tri-Cameral quorum review.

Sacred Pause is **proposal-specific**, not system-wide. Other proposals that do not depend on the missing or ambiguous evidentiary element continue to flow through the Governance Lane and receive their own determinations without delay. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md) This architectural choice is critical for adversarial robustness: if Sacred Pause were system-wide, adversaries could weaponize evidentiary gaps to induce denial-of-service across entire financial markets by deliberately introducing data anomalies. By isolating the pause to the specific proposal, TL maintains systemic continuity while enforcing constitutional prudence. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

The hardware substrate enforces the distinction between state and workflow with physical precision. The Mandated Ternary memristive cells maintain the intermediate resistance state that corresponds to Epistemic Hold regardless of what the Sacred Pause workflow is doing at any given moment. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md) The workflow may be querying data, escalating to human reviewers, or requesting additional model validation. Throughout all of this activity, the MT gate remains physically in the intermediate state. It will not transition until the Governance Lane issues a new cryptographic signature validated by the required Tri-Cameral quorum. The hardware makes the state immutable even while the workflow is highly dynamic.

The Decision Log for an Epistemic Hold must record both the state determination and the Sacred Pause workflow activity. The log contains the initial evidentiary gap that triggered the hold, the supplementary data queries issued, the responses received, the escalation notifications sent, and the final state transition when the uncertainty is resolved. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md) This comprehensive logging ensures that regulators can audit not only the final determination but also the diligence exercised during the pause.

## 2.4 NL=NA: The Non-Negotiable Architectural Covenant

NL=NA is the irreducible axiom of Ternary Logic. It stands for **No Log equals No Action**. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

This is not a preference, a best practice, or a configurable compliance policy. It is a constitutional law embedded at every layer of the framework: application logic, cryptographic infrastructure, governance quorum, and hardware gates. If a proposal does not generate a complete, schema-validated, cryptographically sealed Decision Log, it cannot produce a Permission Token. If it cannot produce a Permission Token, the Gateway cannot open. If the Gateway cannot open, the action does not execute. The chain is absolute and unbroken. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

The Decision Log is not a simple transaction record. It is a structured evidentiary object that contains the original proposal from the Inference Lane, the constitutional evaluation performed by the Governance Lane, the triadic state determination, the evidentiary hash chain, the schema validation checksum, the timestamped cryptographic signatures from the relevant governance chambers, and the full Sacred Pause workflow history if Epistemic Hold was invoked. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md) Every field in the log is schema-mandatory. A missing field causes schema validation failure. Schema validation failure causes Permission Token denial. Permission Token denial causes Gateway lock.

NL=NA is enforced through five distinct and sequential operational layers. A failure at any layer causes automatic termination of the execution path. The layers are not optional modules that an institution can disable for convenience. They are hard-coded into the API specification, the hardware substrate, and the constitutional schema. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md) The five layers - Schema Validation, Cryptographic Integrity, Hardware Enforcement, Governance Quorum, and Regulatory Nexus - are specified in full in Section 3 of this monograph, and their adversarial stress testing is addressed in Section 10.

The second layer, cryptographic sealing, prevents retroactive tampering. The Decision Log is hashed and anchored to the Immutable Ledger before any execution occurs. A regulator examining a central bank's algorithmic decisions five years later can verify that the Decision Log presented today is byte-for-byte identical to the log generated at the moment of decision. Any modification, even a single bit, breaks the hash chain and renders the log cryptographically invalid. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

The third layer, hardware enforcement, closes the gap that software-only governance cannot close. The Mandated Ternary memristive cells in the Governance Lane physically link execution to log verification. The MT gate maintains its intermediate resistance state until it receives a verified confirm pulse derived from the logging lane. This pulse is generated only after the Decision Log has been written, hashed, anchored, and schema-validated. [[4]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/MT_Hardware/MT_System_Architecture_and_Economics.md) Even if a privileged process in the Inference Lane attempts to bypass the Governance Lane entirely, the hardware will not transition to the low-resistance Proceed state without the log-derived pulse. The physical substrate makes NL=NA tamper-evident at the electron level.

The fourth layer, governance quorum, prevents single-actor capture. The Permission Token requires cryptographic signatures from all three chambers of the Tri-Cameral model. The Technical Council signs that the inference payload was evaluated against technically valid criteria. The Stewardship Custodians sign that the evaluation adhered to the Goukassian Vow and the constitutional schema. The Smart Contract Treasury signs that the resources and incentives for the action have been properly allocated. [[3]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Governance/Ternary_Logic_as_Constitutional_Triadic_Governance_Architecture_for_High-Stakes_Automated_Decision-Making.md) Without all required signatures, the Permission Token is incomplete. An incomplete token is treated by the Gateway as a non-existent token.

The ultimate purpose of NL=NA is the redistribution of constitutional power. Under traditional governance, a rogue executive can instruct a trading desk to bypass risk controls during a volatile market session. The architecture permits this. Under TL, the hardware will not permit it. The cryptographic signatures will not permit it. The immutable ledger will not permit it. [[3]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Governance/Ternary_Logic_as_Constitutional_Triadic_Governance_Architecture_for_High-Stakes_Automated_Decision-Making.md) The architecture removes the possibility of executive override by making the log a physical prerequisite for execution.

Power cannot lie because power cannot execute without evidence. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

## 2.5 The Goukassian Principle: Lantern, Signature, and License

The Goukassian Principle is embedded as Pillar III of the Eight Pillars architecture. It mandates that no economic action may execute without a complete, schema-validated, cryptographically sealed Decision Log - operationalizing NL=NA at the protocol level. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

The Principle is architecturally and constitutionally distinct from the Goukassian Vow. The Vow is the constitutional command. The Goukassian Principle is the engineering specification that makes the command computable. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md) Three concrete artifacts instantiate the Principle.

**The Lantern** functions as the framework's integrity self-test and public compliance beacon. It continuously verifies that the core mandates of the Vow are active and operative within the deployment, and broadcasts this status to external monitors. If core mandates are suppressed - whether through institutional pressure, governance capture, or technical compromise - the Lantern automatically revokes its attestation, signaling to the ecosystem that the implementation has lost constitutional legitimacy. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md)

**The Signature** embeds unbreakable provenance into every legitimate implementation. It cryptographically binds the codebase, the constitutional schema version, and the deployment configuration to a specific steward identity, creating a chain of custody that persists through updates, migrations, and forks. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md) Any fork that removes or alters the Signature cannot claim constitutional legitimacy under TL. The Signature is the technical mechanism that makes the License enforceable: a stripped fork is identifiable by the absence or modification of the Signature.

**The License** imposes binding legal prohibitions on framework usage and performs a critical inversion of the standard evidentiary burden: missing logs constitute presumptive evidence of negligence. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md) Under conventional software licenses, the user bears the burden of proving their use complies. Under the TL License, the implementer bears the burden of proving their implementation satisfies the constitutional criteria. A court evaluating a TL dispute need not determine whether the defendant intentionally violated the License. It need only determine whether the required Decision Logs exist. If the logs are absent, the violation is established as a matter of law.

The three artifacts are interdependent. The Lantern depends on the Signature to verify that the implementation it monitors is legitimate. The Signature depends on the License to deter stripping and unauthorized forks. The License depends on the Lantern to detect when an implementation has lost constitutional legitimacy. [[6]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/Adversarial_Survivability/TL_Constitutional_Survivability_Under_Adversarial_Pressure.md) Compromise of any one artifact degrades the others. The adversarial stress testing of each artifact - including confidence poisoning of the Lantern, coerced issuance of the Signature, and semantic drift of the License over five-, ten-, and twenty-year horizons - is assessed in Section 10.

[[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

**Pillar II - Immutable Ledger:** Provides the append-only, tamper-evident evidentiary repository that records every Decision Log, Permission Token, and Governance Lane determination with cryptographic finality. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

## 2.7 Constitutional Governance: What TL Is and Is Not

The architecture described in this Section constitutes a fourth category of institutional governance that is distinct from the three categories identified by institutional economics: markets, hierarchies, and hybrids. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

Markets rely on price signals and competition to discipline opportunism, but fail when information is asymmetric and verification is costly. Hierarchies rely on administrative authority to resolve disputes, but fail when authority is captured, corrupted, or overwhelmed by complexity. Hybrids attempt to combine market incentives with hierarchical oversight, but fail when the oversight mechanisms themselves become subject to the same frictions they were designed to solve. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

Ternary Logic is **constitutional computation**. It is not a market, because it does not rely on price signals to govern high-stakes decisions. It is not a hierarchy, because it does not rely on administrative discretion. It is not a hybrid contract, because it does not depend on ex-post enforcement by courts or regulators. It is a computational institution that embeds the terms of governance into the physical architecture of decision-making, making violation physically impossible rather than merely legally punishable. [[2]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/The%20Architecture%20Of%20Assured%20Governance%20for%20Global%20Economic%20Systems.md)

Three boundaries define what TL is not.

TL is not a replacement for binary computation. Binary inference engines provide the speed, pattern recognition, and statistical throughput essential to modern economic systems at scale. TL operates as a sovereign governance coprocessor in parallel. The two paradigms occupy physically and logically separated lanes that never merge into a single execution path. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

TL is not a scoring system. It does not output probability assessments, risk grades, or conditional approvals. It issues constitutional determinations from a closed three-state alphabet. The Governance Lane evaluates proposals against the Goukassian Vow and returns Proceed, Refuse, or Epistemic Hold. There are no intermediate outputs. [[1]](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/FractonicMind/TernaryLogic/refs/heads/main/API/Specification_Architecture.md)

TL is not a compliance layer. Compliance layers are applied after decisions are made and actions are taken. TL is applied before. The Decision Log is a prerequisite for execution, not a record of execution. The evidentiary infrastructure is a gateway condition, not a post-hoc artifact.
