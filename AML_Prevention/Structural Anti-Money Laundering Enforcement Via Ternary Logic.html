I. Why AML Systems Fail

Contemporary AML architectures suffer from a fundamental Binary Model Brittleness. By forcing every transaction into an "Allow" or "Deny" bucket, systems are forced to accommodate ambiguity in the "Allow" state to preserve liquidity. This creates an Interdiction Gap: the time between a dirty transaction and its post-hoc reporting (SAR).

  • 01. Post-Hoc Reporting: Documentation occurs after funds have moved, rendering enforcement purely archaeological.
  • 02. Silent Overrides: Without immutable pre-action logs, internal controls are bypassed for VIPs.
  • 03. Alert Fatigue: 95%+ false positive rates desensitize compliance officers to genuine risk.

IV. Architecture at Scale

To resolve the tension between high-speed finance (HFT) and rigorous evidence, TL employs a Dual-Lane Latency Architecture and Merkle Batching.

B Merkle Batching (O(1) Scaling)

Anchoring every transaction individually is $O(n)$ and too slow. TL groups thousands of Decision Logs into a single Merkle Tree, anchoring only the Root Hash. This achieves constant time $O(1)$ verification overhead regardless of volume.
