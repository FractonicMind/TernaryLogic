The 4th Pillar: Decision Logs

The Operational Forensic Substrate of Ternary Logic

Purpose of Decision Logs

A critical system requires a verifiable "why" behind every action. Decision Logs (DLs) are not mere records; they are the mechanism that creates immutable causality, preventing retrospective games and enabling true, cross-jurisdictional accountability.

1. Create Causality

DLs bind inputs, actors, models, and reasoning to a timestamped signature *before* an action can occur. This creates a verifiable "why," not just a recording of "what."

2. Prevent Tampering

Immutability by design prevents retrospective reconstruction, memory-holing, or policy laundering. The "why" cannot be altered to fit an outcome after the fact.

3. Enable Accountability

By binding evidence to outcomes on an immutable ledger, DLs provide the forensic proof required for regulatory compliance (FATF, Basel, SEC) and dispute resolution globally.

Architecture & Principles

Dual-Lane Architecture

An "Action Lane" (sub-300ms execution) runs parallel to an "Evidence Lane" (DL writing). The DL must be written *before* the action is committed, ensuring forensic integrity without sacrificing performance.

GDPR & Privacy

Personal data is removed before hashing. DLs contain only pseudonymous identifiers and commitments. This preserves evidentiary continuity while allowing for the right to erasure.

Trade Secrets & EKR

The DL references the model invoked, but the logic is protected by Ephemeral Key Rotation (EKR). Keys vanish after the Hold, leaving permanent proof of *what* ran without exposing *how* it ran.
