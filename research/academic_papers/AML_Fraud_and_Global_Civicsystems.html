Ternary Logic (TL) as Sovereign-Grade Evidentiary Infrastructure

This interactive application explores the architecture, governance, and application of Ternary Logic (TL) for establishing sovereign-grade accountability across global civicsystems, with a focus on AML, fraud, and public safety.

What is TL?

TL is a neutral audit infrastructure, not a policy. It establishes a verifiable, immutable, and auditable decision-chain for high-risk systemic actions, ensuring any institution can prove what it knew when it acted.

Why is it Needed?

Current post-hoc auditing systems fail to prevent sophisticated financial crime, supply-chain forgery, and regulatory failures. They lack the real-time evidentiary framework to enforce accountability *before* irreversible harm occurs.

How does it Work?

By enforcing core principles like the "Epistemic Hold" (a pause on uncertainty) and the "Goukassian Principle" (proof of knowledge), TL creates an evidentiary packet for every material decision, protected by a hybrid cryptographic and human-governance shield.

Governance, Oversight, and Resilience

TL's governance is designed to prevent institutional capture and guarantee continuity. It separates technical evolution, human oversight, and autonomous enforcement into three distinct bodies.

Technical Council

Responsible for the evolution of the TL specification, cryptography, interoperability, and anchoring standards. They manage the technical roadmap and ensure cryptographic agility.

Stewardship Custodians

Independent, multi-national human guardians preventing institutional capture. They oversee key rotation, quorum logic for critical changes, and act as the ultimate human failsafe.

Smart Contract Safeguard

Autonomous guardrails that enforce the non-alterability of TL's core principles. This layer guarantees continuity and ensures no council or operator can "vote TL out of existence."

Adversarial Threat Model & Mitigations +

TL is designed to be adversarially-aware. Key threats and their mitigations include:

  • Threat: Flooding/Spam Attestations.
    Mitigation: Rate limiting, ensemble checks for anomaly detection, and economic staking (where applicable) for oracles.
  • Threat: Oracle/Attestator Corruption.
    Mitigation: Use of diverse, multi-party oracles. Requiring attestations from multiple independent sources before resolving a hold.
  • Threat: Custodian Collusion or Coercion.
    Mitigation: Geographically and jurisdictionally diverse custodians. Multi-party ERK (Ephemeral Key) escrow. Quorum logic requiring super-majority.
  • Threat: Cryptographic Break.
    Mitigation: Cryptographic agility policy managed by the Technical Council, allowing for protocol-level upgrades. Re-anchoring procedures for existing ledgers.

Phased Implementation Roadmap

A gradual rollout is recommended to validate, calibrate, and build institutional acceptance.

  1. Phase 1: Proof of Concept (PoC) & Calibration.
    Deploy TL in a sandboxed environment against historical datasets (e.g., past SARs, trade invoices) to calibrate hold-triggers and ensemble models. KPIs: Trigger accuracy, false positive rate vs. existing models.
  2. Phase 2: Corridor Pilot.
    Deploy TL on a single, live, high-risk corridor (e.g., a specific trade route, one payment rail between two institutions) in parallel with existing systems. KPIs: Mean time to resolution for holds, SAR quality score, operator feedback.
  3. Phase 3: Regional / Sovereign Adoption.
    Scale to a full regional system or national-level infrastructure (e.g., national FIU, trade-finance platform, or RTGS system). KPIs: Audit completeness, regulator acceptance time, system-wide reduction in fraud.

Integration with Existing Systems

TL is an overlay, not a replacement. It integrates via APIs and message queues with existing infrastructure.

  • SIEM/AML Engines: TL triggers (holds) feed into existing case management systems as high-fidelity alerts. TL Decision Logs become admissible evidence attached to the case.
  • Payment Switches (SWIFT, RTGS): TL sits as a pre-processing gateway or post-transaction auditor for high-value payments, enforcing holds *before* settlement where latency budgets permit.
  • Trade-Finance Platforms: Integrates to receive and hash trade documents (B/L, invoices), using oracles to cross-validate data with customs and shipping manifests.
  • National FIUs & Regulators: Provides a "Regulator UI" for lawfully accessing (via ERK) and auditing forensic packets related to investigations.
