Economic Attack Surfaces & Vulnerabilities

Cross-Chain Governance Attacks

Cross-chain governance attacks are the natural evolution of flash loan governance exploits. They're harder to detect because the attack vectors span multiple blockchain networks simultaneously.

A simple form of governance attack is to accumulate enough voting power to pass a malicious proposal that upgrades the protocol to attacker-controlled logic, potentially compromising treasury funds.

Critical Risk: Cross-chain attacks leverage flash loans to temporarily acquire massive voting power across multiple chains, making them extremely difficult to detect and mitigate.

Temporal Safeguards

Flash loan attacks have demonstrated the critical importance of temporal safeguards, with research showing that attackers can exploit protocol vulnerabilities within a single transaction block.

Aave, a decentralized lending and borrowing protocol, introduces innovations like flash loans and dynamic interest rates, enhancing user flexibility and capital efficiency while introducing new attack vectors.

Temporal vulnerabilities arise when protocols fail to implement proper time-based checks, allowing attackers to execute multiple actions within a single block.
